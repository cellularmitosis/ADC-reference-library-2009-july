Binding Elements

To bind an item in your component to a key:

  • Drag from the key to the item, and choose the attribute you want to bind from the menu that appears.

    A black line appears as you drag, And when you drag over an item you can bind to, a black border appears around it.

You can also bind an element's attributes by typing in the Inspector directly. To do this:

  • In the item's Inspector, choose Dynamic Inspector from the pop-up menu at the top left corner, and double-click the Binding column beside the attribute you want to set.

    If the Binding column contains a downward-pointing triangle, you can select the attribute's value by clicking the triangle and choosing an item from the menu that appears.

    As you type, WebObjects Builders tries to complete the binding with keys from the object browser.

    When typing, keep the following in mind:
    • Constant strings (such as "Joe") must be in double quotes.
    • Variable, method names, and symbolic constants (such as joe or true) must not be in quotes.
    • Keys must specify a full key path. For example, to bind a key to the count method of the allGuests member of the application object, enter application.allGuests.count.

Managing Bindings

To delete an attribute's binding:

  • Click the attribute's row and press Delete.

    The attribute's binding is deleted, but the attribute remains in the Inspector.

To view documentation on an item and its bindings:

  • Click the Book button .

To correct binding errors:

  • Make sure none of the attribute's names are written in red. Click the Errors button to see a detailed explanation of the problems.

To add a new attribute:

  • Click the +/- icon in the upper right corner of the Inspector, and choose Add Binding.

    Generally, you don't add attributes to dynamic elements. For standard dynamic elements such as text fields and strings, the attributes always appear in the Inspector. For your own custom WebObjects, use the API editor (see Choosing Attributes for a Component).
